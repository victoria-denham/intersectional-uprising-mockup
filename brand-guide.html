03 Typography
Three Typefaces. One System.

Each typeface has a distinct role. They do not compete. Cormorant gives editorial depth. Bebas gives graphic weight. DM Sans gives clarity.

Editorial / Human
Cormorant Garamond

Always italic. This is the voice of the movement — the feeling behind the action. Never used for utility text. Never upright. Always expressive.

Hero labels and introductions
Pull quotes and founding principles
Nav brand text (top line, small)
Instagram quote tiles
Italic subheadings within sections
Graphic / Bold
Bebas Neue

All caps, always. The graphic layer. Used where weight and presence matter — campaign statements, section headings, statistics, navigation labels.

Hero h1 strong (large display)
Section headings (h2)

Stat and countdown numbers
Instagram headline tiles
City labels over photography

04 Voice and Tone
Direct. Urgent. Human.

IU speaks like a person who means it. Not a campaign. Not a charity. Not a corporation. A movement that is already happening and does not need your permission.

Write like this

  • Short sentences. One idea at a time.
  • Active voice. Things happen. People act. Rights matter.
  • Name the problem directly. Do not soften it.
  • First person plural. We march. We organise. We show up.
  • Give people something concrete to do.
  • Let silence do work. White space between ideas is intentional.

Not like this

  • Empowering. Inspiring. Passionate. Driving change. These are filler words with no weight.
  • Passive constructions. "Rights are being fought for." By whom?
  • Charity-speak. IU is not asking for your sympathy. It is asking for your presence.
  • Jargon, buzzwords, or corporate framing of justice as a "mission statement".
  • Overly long captions. If it needs three paragraphs of setup, cut the setup.
Use

"Women's rights are human rights. Full stop."

Not this

"We are passionately committed to empowering women and driving meaningful change across communities."

Use

"14 cities. One day. Thousands of us."

Not this

"We are thrilled to announce that Intersectional Uprising will be hosting simultaneous marches in 14 locations."

Photography treatment

Desaturation

All march photography is desaturated. Use saturate(0.5–0.6) and brightness(0.7–0.8). The brand colour system should read across the feed — not compete with warm photography tones.

filter: saturate(0.6) brightness(0.8)
Colour overlay

Each photo tile has a gradient overlay that bleeds from the section colour. Violet for tile 2, green for tile 5, black for tile 8. This ties photography back into the brand palette and prevents the grid from feeling inconsistent.

background: linear-gradient(to top, rgba(45,27,78,0.7) 0%, transparent 50%)
City label

Every photo tile gets a city label in Bebas Neue, bottom-left, 0.75rem. This is not a caption — it is part of the tile. It reminds the viewer that this is a specific chapter, a real place, a real march.

Bebas Neue — 0.75rem — letter-spacing 0.08em — parchment
