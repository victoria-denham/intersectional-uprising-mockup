Who We Are

Liberation is collective.

Intersectional Uprising is a grassroots activist collective united in the fight for women's rights. We operate through demonstration, education, and community engagement to advance equity and justice.

We centre marginalised communities: women in Afghanistan, Iran, Palestine, and the United States. Trans women, women of colour, disabled women, migrants, and working-class women. We recognise that overlapping forms of oppression cannot be addressed one at a time.

We will not rest until equity, dignity, justice, and freedom are realities for all women.

Intersectionality — feminism must be intersectional to be meaningful Inclusive spaces — all genders welcome, united by shared commitment Safety and accountability — zero tolerance for discrimination or hate Collective action — because liberation is never individual

Solidarity Marches

We march. Twice a year. Nationwide.

Our twice-yearly nationwide solidarity marches bring together activists from across the UK to amplify silenced voices and demonstrate collective power. Follow our social channels for upcoming march dates and locations.

✈

Nationwide reach

We coordinate marches across the UK with city leads in Manchester, Leeds, Birmingham, London, Brighton, Cardiff, Cambridge, and more.

💪

All are welcome

Female, male, non-binary, and trans individuals — all united by a shared commitment to justice and the belief that liberation is collective.

📣

Organised and safe

Every march is planned with care. We maintain zero tolerance for discrimination, harassment, or hate in all our spaces.

🌏

Global solidarity

We march in solidarity with women in Afghanistan, Iran, Palestine, the United States, and everywhere that women's rights are under threat.
