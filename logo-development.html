05 — Wings only

The bird without the body. What is universally read as phoenix is the wing span — removing the body makes it more symbol than illustration. Scales well. Works embroidered, stamped, printed. The feather tips give it detail at large sizes that simplifies cleanly at small.

19 — Y-form / junction

Three paths become one, and that one rises. The convergence junction is the most literal rendering of intersectionality as a mark. Structurally it also reads as a person with arms raised — the uprising posture. Extremely versatile at all sizes. Arguably the most durable of the four.

23 — Flame iris

Reads simultaneously as an eye (witness, justice, vigilance) and a flame (the fire the phoenix rises from). The ringed structure gives it badge quality — works as a seal or stamp. The most contained of the four. Loses the most detail at very small sizes due to the ring structure.

Hybrid — the brief in one mark

Wings made of strands, all rising from a single point. The phoenix form is implied rather than illustrated. Multiple lines, different weights, converging and ascending together — that is intersectionality, that is uprising, that is the phoenix. Carries the full brief without depicting any of it literally. The varied stroke weights mean it reads at all sizes: at 16px it's a bold fan shape; at large scale the strand structure is visible. This is the direction to develop further.
